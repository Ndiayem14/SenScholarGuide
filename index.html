Understanding Application Costs

Applying to U.S. universities and scholarships incurs several expenses. These include application fees, standardized test fees (SAT, ACT, TOEFL, IELTS,Duolingo), and charges for sending transcripts and documents translation. Students may also need to consider costs for courier services and document translation.Awareness of these costs is essential for effective budgeting during the application process.

Preparing Your Application

Starting the college application process early is key. Ideally, Beginning the college application process in your junior year of high school is crucial. Starting early, like crafting a strong personal statement and securing teacher recommendations, simplifies the process and minimizes stress. This early preparation ensures you meet application deadlines with ease and present your best self to colleges.

Timeframe

The college application process is a marathon, not a sprint, requiring careful time management. Based on my personal experience, dedicating approximately 20 hours each week over several months was essential. This time was invested in preparing for standardized tests like Duolingo and TOEFL, meticulously filling out my Common app Application, and ensuring all documents were accurately translated.

Networking

Networking is a key tool for educational success. Our platform facilitates connections with peers, alumni, and professionals, creating a supportive community. These connections offer opportunities to share knowledge and resources, which can significantly impact your educational journey. By engaging with this network, you can access valuable guidance and support, paving your way to success.

Steps

  • Search for universities

    Explore universities offering full scholarships or practicing need-blind admissions.

  • Ask your Teachers for recommendations

    Securing a strong recommendation is a crucial aspect of the application process. Often, teachers may be unfamiliar with submitting recommendations via the Common App website. A helpful guide for teachers on creating a profile in the Common App can be found in the resources section.

  • Fill out the Common App

    The Common App platform facilitates applications to numerous universities, making it essential to meticulously manage the information provided. Of particular importance are the extracurricular activities section, which plays a critical role in the application.

  • Initiate your essays writing Journey

    Begin drafting your personal statement and essays early, ensuring ample time to refine these crucial components of your application. Engaging in this process promptly will allow for thoughtful development and necessary revisions.

  • Ask your Teachers for recommendations

    Securing a strong recommendation is a crucial aspect of the application process. Often, teachers may be unfamiliar with submitting recommendations via the Common App website. A helpful guide for teachers on creating a profile in the Common App can be found in the resources section.

  • Understand the Application Requirements

    International students must thoroughly review and adhere to each college's specific requirements. This includes completing detailed application forms, submitting academic transcripts (often needing translation), crafting compelling essays and personal statements, and obtaining relevant recommendations.

  • Englis Proficiency Test

    An English proficiency test is necessary because the Senegalese education system is in French, and the universities need to be secure in our fluency in English. The test is significantly more accessible than the SAT but expensive. Duolingo is cheaper but most universities do not accept it. In advice, you can see some cheaper alternatives.

  • Submit your applications

    Consider whether you'll be applying via early decision, early action, or regular decision, and submit your application accordingly. Always review your application thoroughly, ideally more than once, and have others read your essays to provide feedback for potential improvements. Most importantly, review your application before submitting it.

  • Translate your grades and Legal documents

    One of the more challenging aspects involves translating all academic documents, including the Baccalaureate diploma and grade reports (Bulletin de notes). For further details, please refer to the advice section.

Advices

Sift, then apply.

1st Advice

Begin your scholarship search well in advance of application deadlines. This gives you ample time to prepare your application and gather necessary documents.Moreover, some scholarships have early bird deadlines that come with additional benefits, so being ahead of schedule could be really helpful on the process.

2nd Advice

Explore various scholarship options, including those offered by U.S. colleges,government programs, international organizations, and private foundations.Additionally, consider special-interest scholarships that may align with your background, such as those for students in specific fields of study or with unique life expriences.

3dr Advice

If you haven't been practicing English recently, concentrate on excelling in the math section, which doesn't rely heavily on language skills. The writing section may also be more straightforward for you. Often, the reading section is where most errors occur, so aim to be as accurate as possible in math to balance this out.

4th Advice

Highlight unique aspects of your Background by emphasizing what makes you unique, such as your cultural background, community involvement, or specific achievements and experiences. Detail how these elements have shaped your perspective and prepared you for the academic journey ahead in the United States.

5th Advice

Maintain academic excellence, keep your grades up, as many scholarships have a minimum GPA requirement. Consistently good grades reflect your dedication and can also open doors to more exclusive scholarship opportunities with stringent academic standards.

6th Advice

Aim to achieve a high TOEFL score, ideally over 100 points, as it demonstrates to universities your proficiency in English and ability to excel in standardized testing environments. A strong TOEFL score is often a key criterion in admissions and scholarship decisions.

TOEFL

The TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language) is a standardized test assessing the English skills of non-native speakers for academic purposes. It covers reading, writing, listening, and speaking. Essential for international students, a good TOEFL score is often required to study in English-speaking universities.
